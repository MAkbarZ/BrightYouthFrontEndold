What Is Bookkeeping?

Bookkeeping is tracking and recording the financial transactions or financial events of a business organization.

Financial Transaction

An exchange of goods or a value of money value.

We usually receive a benefit from someone or providing benefit to someone. When we measure those benefits (goods or service) in terms of money value, it is referred to as a financial activity or a financial transaction.


Explanation of System

A system is the combination of Processes achieving a Goal.

- Many tasks (similar or different) are aligned/chained up in a process.
- Many processes (similar or different) are aligned/chained up in a system.

  • - Purchase or sale of goods.
  • - Buying Assets e.g. Land, building or machinery, etc.
  • - Paying Expenses e.g. Rent, salaries, insurance, taxes, etc.
  • - Realizing of Bad Debt, when debtors doesn't answer your mails and you forgive them.

These are the few examples of financial transactions which are an act to provide or receive benefits or both.

Financial Event

An occurrence of having any benefit or loss to a business organization, of something of money value.

Here are few examples:

  • - Provision for bad debt – the bad debts which have not happened yet; we are just cautious if it happens.
  • - Depreciation – We can’t recognize actual damages or wear and tear caused in an asset, but we anticipate, what if our asset needs any repair or change of spare parts shall require in the future, or just major change happens which can cause in loss of value in the asset, such as brand change, energy consumption problem, etc.
  • - Exchange rate gain or loss.
  • - Price fluctuation of goods or assets.
  • - Fine or Dues.
