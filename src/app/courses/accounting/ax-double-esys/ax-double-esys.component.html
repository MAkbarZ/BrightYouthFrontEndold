The accounting equation provides a concept of having dual-aspect record for each single financial transaction or event. The accounting equation also advises us that in every single transaction, there is always two effects.

On one side, we receive something, e.g. asset, goods or service. On the other side, and at the same time, we are also paying through Cash or bank, or a having a claim from the person or business to whom we are having the transaction.

This happens in a single financial event as well. When we buy an insurance plan, for example, for 2 years which we record as prepaid (an asset) at the end of 1st year, we realize that we have used 1st year’s portion of the insurance plan, by considering that we have gained enough attraction of the public towards our product and services. Thus, we decrease Prepaid Insurance Plan, and Increase Insurance Expense.

Account Type Increase Decrease Explanation
Asset Benefit Loss The business records as benefit when an asset (for example, land, building, cash, or claim on someone or another business) is received, otherwise, it is not in the benefit of business that its’ assets have been decreased.
Owner’s Equity Loss Benefit The business records as loss when owner’s claim over the asset of the business increases.

This means that at any time in the future, owner(s) might close the business, or at least, resize-down the business by withdrawing money or money equivalent from the business.
Liabilities Loss Benefit Like owner’s equity, the business also records a loss when liability has increased. This means that claim of lender has been established or increased over the asset of the business. If liability has been decreased, by paying off to the lender, the business records as benefit.
Income Loss Benefit Students always confuse over a concept of why the business records Income (or Sales) as a loss.
  1. Goods delivered to Customer.
    Business records a loss. (Credited)

  2. Profit margin agreed by customer to pay to business (who is seller in this case).
    >Business records an increase in owner’s equity, to pay to owner in the future. (Credited)

  3. Cash is received from customer, or the customer will pay in the future.
    Cash or Receivable increases. (Debited)

For simplicity, in the beginning of accounting studies, point 1 and 2 above are combined and just referred to a single term as “Sales”.
Expenses Benefit Loss The business always enjoys if its resources are increased, more building area, machine hours, labor hours, experts’ hours, insurances, bank services, stationaries, equipment, etc.

While students confuse here that expenses are bad, but it is not. Expenses are resources which is benefit for the business. “Paying for those Expenses” lead to decrease in Cash or Asset, which is loss.

More resources you put in the business, more performances arise in generation of revenues through best product and services. As resources are scarce, it is expenses, and business must pay more and more to acquire sufficient resources.

One other aspect of Expenses is that it reduces Owner’s Equity, that’s why, students think of it as “Loss”.
  • - It is Benefit for the Business.

  • - It is Loss for the Owner

An Accounting System always focuses on business’ benefits while analyzing a transaction, but while making financial statements, the accountant’s considers stakeholders as high priority.
