Cash Flow Statement is prepared to show the flow of cash in and out of a business during a period.

A Cash flow statement shows from which sources cash comes in, and to where cash has been spent.

A Cash flow statement has following Three parts:

  1. Cash flow from Operating Activities.
  2. Cash flow from Investing Activities.
  3. Cash flow from Financing Activities.

1- Cash Flow from Operating Activities

Operating Activities of a business refers to generation of income by using resources (expenses).

Thus, this part of the “Statement of Cash Flow” shows:

  1. Cash inflow: by receiving of cash from sales and other income.
  2. Cash outflow: by paying for expenses during the reporting period.

2- Cash Flow from Investing Activities

Investing Activities of a business refers to acquisition and disposal of assets, purchasing an investment and receipt of dividend or bonuses.

Thus, this part of the “Statement of Cash Flow” shows:

  1. Cash inflow: by receiving of cash from disposal of non-current asset and/or receipt from return on investment.
  2. Cash outflow: by paying for acquisition of non-current asset and investment.

3- Cash Flow from Financing Activities

Financing Activities of a business refers to investment by owner, paying dividend, receiving bank loan, payment of loan installment, and/or issuance of shares, paying bonuses or dividends to shareholders.

Thus, this part of the “Statement of Cash Flow” shows:

  1. Cash inflow: by receiving due to investment by owner, receiving bank loan, and/or issuance of shares.
  2. Cash outflow: by paying for dividend, loan installment, bonuses or dividends to shareholders.
