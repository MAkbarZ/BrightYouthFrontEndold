Closing Entries are a set journal entries recorded after certain events are recognized by an accountant after the preparation of a trial balance.

The purpose is to merge the balances of those accounts into a single account whose balances are not supposed to be transferred to next accounting period.

There are mainly 5 set of closing entries which are as follows:

  1. Closing Revenue accounts.
  2. Closing Purchase accounts.
  3. Closing Expense accounts.
  4. Closing Profit account.
  5. Closing Withdrawal account.

1. Closing Revenue Accounts

Following journal entry is passed to close all the revenue accounts.


GENERAL JOURNAL
Transaction Date Transaction Type Account Name L.F Debit
(Rupees)		 Credit
(Rupees)
Dec 31. 2022 REVENUE Sales XXXX
SUMMARY Income Summary XXXX
Revenue accounts closed for the year ending Dec 31, 20XX

Purpose: Sales amount is the figure to show for One accounting period only. Sales amount has contributed to One accounting period’s profit only. It has nothing to contribute in past or future profits.

That’s why we have recorded this entry to show zero sales balance at the end of the accounting period.


2. Closing Purchase Accounts

Following journal entry is passed to close all the purchase accounts.


GENERAL JOURNAL
Transaction Date Transaction Type Account Name L.F Debit
(Rupees)		 Credit
(Rupees)
Dec 31. 2022 SUMMARY Income Summary XXXX
PURCHASE Purchase XXXX
Purchase accounts closed for the year ending Dec 31, 20XX

Purpose: Like Sales, purchase amount is the figure to show for One accounting period only. Purchases amount has contributed in the generated of One accounting period’s profit only. It has nothing to contribute in generation of past or future profits.

That’s why we have recorded this entry to show zero sales balance at the end of the accounting period.


3. Closing Expense Accounts

Following journal entry is passed to close all the purchase accounts.


GENERAL JOURNAL
Transaction Date Transaction Type Account Name L.F Debit
(Rupees)		 Credit
(Rupees)
Dec 31. 2022 SUMMARY Income Summary XXXX
EXPENSE Expense XXXX
Expense accounts closed for the year ending Dec 31, 20XX

Purpose: Like Sales and purchases, expense amount is the figure to show for One accounting period only. Expense amount has contributed in the generated of One accounting period’s profit only. It has nothing to contribute in generation of past or future profits.

That’s why we have recorded this entry to show zero sales balance at the end of the accounting period.


4. Closing Profit (OR Loss) Account

Following journal entry is passed to close all the profit accounts.


GENERAL JOURNAL
Transaction Date Transaction Type Account Name L.F Debit
(Rupees)		 Credit
(Rupees)
Dec 31. 2022 OWNER’S EQUITY Profit XXXX
OWNER’S EQUITY Owner’s Equity XXXX
Profit account closed for the year ending Dec 31, 20XX

Purpose: Profit amount is the figure to show increase in capital for One accounting period only. Profit amount has been generated for One accounting period’s profit only. It has not generated for past or future profits.

That’s why we have recorded this entry to show zero sales balance at the end of the accounting period.

Note: if there is loss, then we will have reversal of the above journal entry, as follows:


GENERAL JOURNAL
Transaction Date Transaction Type Account Name L.F Debit
(Rupees)		 Credit
(Rupees)
Dec 31. 2022 OWNER’S EQUITY Owner’s Equity XXXX
OWNER’S EQUITY Loss XXXX
Loss account closed for the year ending Dec 31, 20XX

5. Closing Withdrawal Accounts

Following journal entry is passed to close all the withdrawal accounts.


GENERAL JOURNAL
Transaction Date Transaction Type Account Name L.F Debit
(Rupees)		 Credit
(Rupees)
Dec 31. 2022 OWNER’S EQUITY Owner’s Equity XXXX
OWNER’S EQUITY Drawings XXXX
Drawings account closed for the year ending Dec 31, 20XX

Purpose: Like profit or loss, Drawing amount is the figure to show decrease in capital for One accounting period only. Drawing amount has been made by the owner for One accounting period only. It has not drawn for past or future year.

That’s why we have recorded this entry to show zero sales balance at the end of the accounting period.
