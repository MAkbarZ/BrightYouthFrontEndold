The accounting equations is described as the demonstration of a business’ financial position into a mathematical equation.

ASSETS = LIABILITIES + OWNER(S)’ EQUITY

Accounting equation is just an evaluation of business’ financial statement. It is neither a formal report, as it is just for accounting students to understand a financial statement; nor it is allowed to be shown in financial statements.

The accounting equation can be re-formed per the different sizes of businesses, as follows:

Purpose Accounting Equation Variations
For a small business: ASSETS = OWNER’S EQUITIES
For a small and medium businesses: ASSETS = LIABILITIES + OWNER’S EQUITIES
For Multi-Owner or Partnership businesses: ASSETS = LIABILITIES + OWNERS’ EQUITIES
For Public Liability or Large businesses: ASSETS = LIABILITIES + STOCKHOLDERS’ EQUITIES

Accounting Equation - Manipulations

As we understand that an Accounting Equation is after all a Mathematical equation and we can apply the mathematical equation rules to find out Assets, Liabilities and Owners’ equity in different situations.

Purpose Accounting Equation Variations
1. Basic Form (i.e. to know total Assets) ASSETS = LIABILITIES + OWNER(S)’ EQUITY
2. To find out Liabilities of a business LIABILITIES = ASSETS - OWNER(S)’ EQUITY
3. To find out Total Owner(s)’ equity OWNER(S)’ EQUITY = ASSETS - LIABILITIES


Example 1: Calculate Assets.

We are provided with below:

Owner’s Equity = Rs. 200,000

Liabilities = Rs. 500,000


Then, applying accounting equation:

Assets = Liabilities + Owner(S)’ Equity


Putting values of Owner’s Equity and Liabilities, we get:

Assets = Rs. 500,000 + Rs. 200,000

Assets = Rs. 700,000


Example 2: Calculate Liabilities.

We are provided with below:

Assets = Rs. 800,000

Owner’s Equity = Rs. 200,0000


Then, applying accounting equation:

Assets = Liabilities + Owner(S)’ Equity

Liabilities = Assets – Owner(s)’ Equity


Putting values of Assets and Owner’s Equity, we get:

Liabilities = Rs. 800,000 - Rs. 200,000

Liabilities = Rs. 600,000


Example 3: Calculate Owner’s Equity.

We are provided with below:

Assets = Rs. 800,000

Liabilities = Rs. 150,000


Then, applying accounting equation:

Assets = Liabilities + Owner(S)’ Equity

Owner(s)’ Equity = Assets – Liabilities


Putting values of Assets and Liabilities, we get:

Owner(s)’ Equity = Rs. 800,000 - Rs. 150,000

Owner(s)’ Equity = Rs. 550,000

Owner(s)’ Equity = Rs. 550,000
