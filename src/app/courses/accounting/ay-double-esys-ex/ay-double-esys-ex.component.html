Exercise 1: Double Entry for Mr. A’s business.

We are provided with below:

  1. Mr. A started a business with Cash Rs. 1,000,000.
  2. Received loan from Bank Rs. 700,000.
  3. Purchased Land Rs. 200,000 and paid Cash in full.
  4. Purchased Building Rs. 300,000 and paid Cash in full.
  5. Purchased Vehicle Rs. 150,000 and paid Cash in full.
  6. Purchased Furniture Rs. 80,000 and paid Cash in full.
  7. Purchased Goods for Resale Rs. 600,000 and paid Cash in full

Solution: Double Entry for Mr. A’s business.


Transaction # Account Type Transaction Effect Increase/ Decrease Amount Remarks
1.1 ASSET Benefit Increase 1,000,000 Business will get benefit from the asset’s usage (i.e. Cash) in the future.
OWNER’S EQUITY Loss Increase 1,000,000 Business will get a loss from the owner’s equity if business is dissolved or when owner withdraw from business.
1.2 ASSET Benefit Increase 700,000 Business will get benefit from the asset’s usage (i.e. Cash) in the future.
LIABILITIES Loss Increase 700,000 Business will get a loss from the liability, because business pays back to the bank in the form of cash or if business is dissolved, business must provide another asset.
1.3 ASSET Benefit Increase 200,000 Business will get benefit from the asset’s usage (i.e. Land) in the future.
ASSET Loss Decrease 200,000 Business has gotten a loss by decreasing another asset (i.e. Cash).
1.4 ASSET Benefit Increase 300,000 Business will get benefit from the asset’s usage (i.e. Building) in the future.
ASSET Loss Decrease 300,000 Business has gotten a loss by decreasing another asset (i.e. Cash).
1.5 ASSET Benefit Increase 150,000 Business will get benefit from the asset’s usage (i.e. Vehicle) in the future.
ASSET Loss Decrease 150,000 Business has gotten a loss by decreasing another asset (i.e. Cash).
1.6 ASSET Benefit Increase 80,000 Business will get benefit from the asset’s usage (i.e. Furniture) in the future.
ASSET Loss Decrease 80,000 Business has gotten a loss by decreasing another asset (i.e. Cash).
1.7 ASSET Benefit Increase 600,000 Business will get benefit from the asset’s usage (i.e. Goods or Inventory) by re-selling it to customers in the future.
ASSET Loss Decrease 600,000 Business has gotten a loss by decreasing another asset (i.e. Cash).



Exercise 2: Mr. A’s business – First Year - Sales and Expenses

We are provided with below:

  1. Sold goods and received Cash Rs. 500,000.
  2. Paid Rent Rs. 80,000.
  3. Paid Salaries Rs. 70,000.
  4. Paid Electricity Bill Rs. 30,000.
  5. Paid Vehicle Fuel Rs. 40,000.
  6. Paid Furniture repair Rs. 12,000.
  7. Paid Building repair Rs. 8,000.
  8. Paid for Stationary Rs. 7,000.

Solution: Double Entry for Mr. A’s business.


Transaction # Account Type Transaction Effect Increase/ Decrease Amount Remarks
2.1 ASSET Benefit Increase 500,000 Business will get benefit from the asset’s usage (i.e. Cash) in the future.
INCOME Loss Increase 500,000 The owner’s equity has been increased; if the owner has withdrawn the money, the business will get lesser amount of Asset (i.e. Cash).
2.2 EXPENSE Benefit Increase 80,000 Business has already received benefits from utilizing the rental premises.
ASSET Loss Decrease 80,000 Business has gotten a loss by decreasing another asset (i.e. Cash).
2.3 EXPENSE Benefit Increase 70,000 Business has already received benefits from utilizing the employees’ efforts.
ASSET Loss Decrease 70,000 Business has gotten a loss by decreasing another asset (i.e. Cash).
2.4 EXPENSE Benefit Increase 30,000 Business has already received benefits from utilizing the electricity.
ASSET Loss Decrease 30,000 Business has gotten a loss by decreasing another asset (i.e. Cash).
2.5 EXPENSE Benefit Increase 40,000 Business has already received benefits from utilizing the fuel for vehicle.
ASSET Loss Decrease 40,000 Business has gotten a loss by decreasing another asset (i.e. Cash).
2.6 EXPENSE Benefit Increase 12,000 Business will get benefits from the furniture, by repairing it for future use.
ASSET Loss Decrease 12,000 Business has gotten a loss by decreasing another asset (i.e. Cash).
2.7 EXPENSE Benefit Increase 8,000 Business will get benefits from the building, by repairing it for future use.
ASSET Loss Decrease 8,000 Business has gotten a loss by decreasing another asset (i.e. Cash).
2.8 EXPENSE Benefit Increase 7,000 Business has already received benefits from utilizing the stationary.
ASSET Loss Decrease 7,000 Business has gotten a loss by decreasing another asset (i.e. Cash).

Transaction # Account Type Transaction Effect Increase/ Decrease Amount Remarks

Exercise 4: Mr. A’s business – First Year - Sales on Credit, Purchase on Credit, Collection of Debt, Payment of Debt.

We are provided with below:

  1. Sold goods Rs. 80,000 still receivable from Customers.
  2. Purchased goods Rs. 90,000 still payable to Suppliers.
  3. Received cash of Rs. 20,000 from a customer, Mr. Ehsan.
  4. Paid cash of Rs. 30,000 to a supplier, XYZ company.

Solution: Double Entry for Mr. A’s business.


Transaction # Account Type Transaction Effect Increase/ Decrease Amount Remarks
4.1 ASSET Benefit Increase 80,000 Business will get Cash from the debtor (i.e. Accounts Receivable) in the future.
INCOME Loss Increase 80,000 The owner’s equity has been increased; if the owner has withdrawn the money, the business will get lesser amount of Asset (i.e. Cash).
4.2 ASSET Benefit Increase 90,000 Business will get benefit from the asset’s usage (i.e. Cash) in the future.
LIABILITIES Loss Increase 90,000 Business will get a loss from the liability, because business pays back to the bank in the form of cash or if business is dissolved, business must provide another asset.
4.3 ASSET Benefit Increase 20,000 Business will get benefit from the asset’s usage (i.e. Cash) in the future.
ASSET Loss Increase 20,000 Business has gotten a loss by decreasing another asset (i.e. Accounts Receivable). Therefore, business will no longer receive cash from debtor in the future.
4.4 LIABILITY Benefit Decrease 30,000 Business will no longer pay to supplier in the future.
ASSET Loss Decrease 30,000 Business has gotten a loss by decreasing another asset (i.e. Cash).
