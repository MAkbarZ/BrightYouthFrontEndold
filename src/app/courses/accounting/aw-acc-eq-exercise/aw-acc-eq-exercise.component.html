Example 1: Starting of a Business

We are provided with below:

  1. Mr. A started a business with Cash Rs. 1,000,000.
  2. Received loan from Bank Rs. 700,000.
  3. Purchased Land Rs. 200,000 and paid Cash in full.
  4. Purchased Building Rs. 300,000 and paid Cash in full.
  5. Purchased Vehicle Rs. 150,000 and paid Cash in full.
  6. Purchased Furniture Rs. 80,000 and paid Cash in full.
  7. Purchased Goods for Resale Rs. 600,000 and paid Cash in full

Solution: Accounting equation for Mr. A’s business.


Transaction # Assets = Liabilities + Owner's Equity
Cash +Land +Building +Vehicle +Furniture +Inventory =Bank +Mr. A
1.1 1,000,000 +0 +0 +0 +0 +0 =0 +1,000,000
1.2 700,000 +0 +0 +0 +0 +0 =700,000 +0
1.3 (200,000) +200,000 +0 +0 +0 +0 =0 +0
1.4 (300,000) +0 +300,000 +0 +0 +0 =0 +0
1.5 (150,000) +0 +0 +150,000 +0 +0 =0 +0
1.6 (80,000) +0 +0 +0 +80,000 +0 =0 +0
1.7 (600,000) +0 +0 +0 +0 +600,000 =0 +0
Sub-total: 370,000 +200,000 +300,000 +150,000 +80,000 +600,000 =700,000 +1,000,000
Grand Total: 1,700,000 = 1,700,000
