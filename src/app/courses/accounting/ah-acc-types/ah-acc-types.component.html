Followings are the six account types which groups all the accounts of a business to summarize and understand the effect of each account while they increase or decrease.

Asset

An asset is a resource of a business, which is owned by the business for the purpose to utilize the benefit in future.

For example, Land, Building, Vehicle, Machinery, Equipment, Investment, Inventory Receivables, Prepaid Expenses, Cash in Hand and in Bank.

Owner’s Equity

Owner’s Equity is an investment of the Owner of a business. Investment is made by owner by introducing a resource/asset in the business.

In return, this investment by the owner arises or increase a claim of the owner over those assets of the business.

Owner’s Drawings

When owner of a business withdraws cash or an asset from the business for his/her personal use.

Liabilities

Obligation to pay someone, other than owner, against their claims over the asset of the business is called liabilities.

The business might have acquired more cash from a bank or financial institution to pay for the resources, which owners couldn’t provide at the time.

Sometimes, the business has acquired an asset and made an agreement with the supplier to provide cash in the future.

Both these dealings are considered a financial transaction which creates liabilities over the business.

Revenue and Income

The words “Revenue” and “Income” are used interchangeably, but they are not same.

Revenue means, the amount of money value which generate by a business through an economic activity.

Income means, the amount of acquisition expenses arises at the time of generating that revenue, removed from revenue amount.

When a business makes sales, there are few costs those sales, for example, discount given, delivery charges, agreement expenses, etc.

An economic activity might include, but not limited to, below:

  • - Sale of goods or services.
  • - Rent of business’ extra resources, such as garage services for other business, conference hall rent, renting a vehicle or machine to other business, etc.
  • - Receipts from investments in another business.
  • - Interest from Banks on fixed deposits or interest-bearing bank accounts.
  • - Interest from someone against loan provided.
  • - etc.

Expenses

Value of resources “already used” by the business during a period of performing an economic activity.

For example,

  • - Rent paid for the month of April 2022. We have used a premises for which we are paying the rent, we have utilized the benefit during that month.
  • - Salaries paid for the month of April 2022. We have already utilized services of employees for during that month.
  • - etc.
