At the end of financial period, we get an Accounting Equation for a single account automatically.

CLOSING BALANCE = OPENING BALANCE + ADDITIONS – DEDUCTIONS



Example 1: Calculate Cash-in-hand.


Note:

- "Closing Balance of Cash" is called Cash-in-Hand.

- Similarly, "Closing Balance of Inventory" is simply called Inventory, Closing Stock, Available Goods or Goods Unsold.

We are provided with below:

Opening Balance = Rs. 0

Total Receipts = Rs. 200,000

Total Payments = Rs. 150,000


Thus,

Closing Balance = Opening Balance + Additions – Deductions

Closing Balance of Cash = Opening Balance + Total Receipts – Total Payments



Putting values of Opening Balance, Total Receipts and Total Payments, we get:

Cash-in-hand = Rs. 0 + Rs. 200,000 - Rs. 150,000

Cash-in-hand = Rs. 50,000


Example 2: Calculate Closing Stock.

We are provided with below:

Opening Balance = Rs. 0

Total Cost of Goods Purchased = Rs. 700,000

Total Cost of Goods Sold = Rs. 450,000


Thus,

Closing Balance = Opening Balance + Additions – Deductions

Closing Balance of Stock = Opening Balance + Total Cost of Goods Purchased – Total Cost of Goods Sold


Putting values of Opening Balance, Total Cost of Goods Purchased and Total Cost of Goods Sold, we get:

Closing Stock = Rs. 0 + Rs. 700,000 - Rs. 450,000

Closing Stock = Rs. 250,000
