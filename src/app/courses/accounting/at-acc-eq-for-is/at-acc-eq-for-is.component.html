In most of the educational materials, it is not found to have an Accounting Equation to study relations between Income, Expenses, Profit or Loss. Thus, we make one.

NET INCOME (A PROFIT OR A LOSS) = TOTAL SALES – TOTAL EXPENSES

To find out, if Net Income is a Profit or a Loss, as follows:

NET INCOME = PROFIT (where Sales is GREATER than Expenses) i.e. (+ve) positive Amount

OR

NET INCOME = LOSS (where Sales is LESSER than Expenses) i.e. (-ve) negative Amount



Example 1: Calculate Net Income.

We are provided with below:

Total Sales = Rs. 400,000

Total Expenses = Rs. 350,000


As, sales is GREATER than Expenses. Thus,

Net Income = Total Sales – Total Expenses


Putting values of Total Sales and Total Expenses, we get:

Net Income = Rs. 400,000 - Rs. 350,000

Net Income = Rs. 50,000

This amount is in Positive value; thus, we can consider:

Net Profit = Rs. 50,000


Example 2: Calculate Net Income.

We are provided with below:

Total Sales = Rs. 380,000

Total Expenses = Rs. 390,000


As, sales is LESSER than Expenses. Thus,

Net Income = Total Sales – Total Expenses


Putting values of Total Sales and Total Expenses, we get:

Net Income = Rs. Rs. 380,000 - Rs. 390,000

Net Income = (Rs. 10,000)

This amount is in Negative value; thus, we can consider:

Net Loss = Rs. 10,000


Note: If we use the term "Loss", then it is wrong to mention the amount in Negative.
