Accountants combine few accounts' processes to achieve a result for another account. Income Statement, Statement of Owner’s Equity and Statement of Cash Flows are also giant accounting equations within themselves.

In Job-order costing, cost of goods sold statement, we will study the accounting equation vertically.



Example 1: Accounting Equation for Cost of Goods Sold Statement

We are provided with below:

Note:

Each of these items are a single accounting equation from within.

For Example:


Sales = Opening Sales + Total Sales for the Period – Total Sales Return for the Period

OR

Raw Material (in Warehouse) = Opening Material + Total Issued to Factory – Total Returned from Factory

OR

Cash Discount = Opening Cash Discount + Total Discount Received – Total Discount Returned

And so on...

Total Sales = Rs. 500,000

Total Raw Material purchased = Rs. 300,000

Total Cash Discount = Rs. 2,000

Total Delivery Charges = Rs. 18,000


Then,

Amount (Rupees)
Total Sales 500,000
Less: Raw Materials (300,000)
Less: Cash Discount (2,000)
Less: Delivery Charges (18,000)
Total Cost of Goods Sold 180,000

We can re-write this statement into an accounting equation as follows:


Total COGS = Total Sales – Raw Materials – Cash Discount – Delivery Charges

Total COGS = Rs. 500,000 – Rs. 300,000 – Rs, 2,000 – Rs. 18,000

Total COGS = Rs. 180,000
