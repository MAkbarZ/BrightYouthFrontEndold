When a seller issues an invoice to its customers, the seller is prohibited to change that invoice for any reason.

Instead, the seller issues credit notes to its customers for the changes required to be done in the invoice.

Note: Formats of invoice, credit notes and debit notes are the same, with just minor differences.

Following are the situations where Credit notes are issued by the seller to buyer/customer.

  1. Whole invoice need to be reversed – for example, full return of the goods accepted by the seller.
  2. Part of the invoice need to be reversed – for example, invoice is incorrect, or goods returned by buyer.
  3. Amendment required in the invoice – for example, tax law changed, further changes, any addition or deduction required, etc.
