Businesses require that their Accountants provide analysis of the past records and at the same time advice for the future impacts of economic situations. Few of the accounting roles are as follows:

Product Pricing Forecast

Accountants provide forecasts and analysis about product pricing of a business in the future, based on economic and market situation. This helps the business to align its production and marketing strategies based on those forecasts.

Budgeting

A business makes a financial plan for their revenue targets and expenditure planning to achieve those targets.

Accountants provide necessary forecast and helps business to make their budgets. Budgets are made for short-term consists of 1 - 12 months duration, and long-term plans which consists of 2 to 5 years duration.

Cash Forecast

A business makes a forecast about requirement and flow of cash throughout budget period. The Business aligns this forecast accordingly when needed.

Investment Planning

A business needs to provide it owner(s) or shareholders with a forecast about available cash or cash equivalent to invest in external business or purchase securities, etc.

Also, if any land or equipment or services is available as extra, business can provide those resources for rental income or fee to other businesses.

Funding

Most businesses require for donation or raise funding from government, public, or business communities time-to-time. They require such fundings if those businesses are required to provide social services to public, or other public sector organizations.

Accountants however are appointed to make budgets for those economic activities and services, as well as make detailed plans on how to spend those fundings.

Accountants also provide data about whether a business requires more investment from shareholders or proceed for bank loan.

Purchase planning

Accountants are required to provide a list of purchases to be made in future. This requires an accountant to collect data regarding asset requirement and monitor available resources for the smooth operation of the business.

Receivables

Accountants are required to maintain active control on receivables and collections from debtors. They are hold responsible to provide on-time reports to avoid Bad Debts or overcome overdues. The accountants provide advice to the business to implement discount packages to debtors such as paying before 30 days results in 1% discount.

Payables

Accountants are required to monitor payables and loans for their on-time installments and interest payments and at the same time, avoid extra fees and penalties. Large and un-reasonable overdues mostly result in legal court proceedings from creditors.

Tax

Businesses hires Tax Accountants to better manage their tax matters and transactions. They are expected to provide guidance and forecasts about future tax changes which might impact over the resources of the business and profitability.

Tax accountants actively comply with tax laws of the country and avoid tax evasions, negligence and errors due to clerical calculations or any mishandling in financial documents and evidence.

Business Closing

For a small business, it is easy to close a business by owner, because not too many financial records are necessary to report to government.

For large businesses, however, there are many complications in the financial information due to large number of transactions and evidence. Government asks huge amount of information and require proper audit from different agencies. For giant businesses, politicians, leaders, other businesses, banks, etc. the public votes are required for such decisions.
