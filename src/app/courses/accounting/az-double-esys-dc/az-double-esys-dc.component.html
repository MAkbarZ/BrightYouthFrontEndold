The double entry system of recording introduces two terms to identify dual impacts in every transaction, i.e. Debit and Credit.

Debit

Debit means the aspect of the transactions in which a business receives any benefit.

Credit

Credit means the aspect of the transactions in which a business receives any loss.


A business’ internal purpose is to acquire and maintain maximum amount of “Economic Resources” such as assets which prolongs life of the business. These resources are crucial and when the business receives or uses them, the accountant of the business considers that event “a benefit” and records as Debit.

On the other hand, the accountant considers those events which threatens the business’ resources from being used.


Now, we simply replace Benefit with “Debit” and Loss with “Credit” words as below.

Account Type Increase Decrease Explanation
Asset Debit Credit The business records as benefit when an asset (for example, land, building, cash, or claim on someone or another business) is received, otherwise, it is not in the benefit of business that its’ assets have been decreased.
Owner’s Equity Credit Debit The business records as loss when owner’s claim over the asset of the business increases.

This means that at any time in the future, owner(s) might close the business, or at least, resize-down the business by withdrawing money or money equivalent from the business.
Liabilities Credit Debit Like owner’s equity, the business also records a loss when liability has increased. This means that claim of lender has been established or increased over the asset of the business. If liability has been decreased, by paying off to the lender, the business records as benefit.
Income Credit Debit Students always confuse over a concept of why the business records Income (or Sales) as a loss.
  1. Goods delivered to Customer.
    Business records a loss. (Credited)

  2. Profit margin agreed by customer to pay to business (who is seller in this case).
    >Business records an increase in owner’s equity, to pay to owner in the future. (Credited)

  3. Cash is received from customer, or the customer will pay in the future.
    Cash or Receivable increases. (Debited)

For simplicity, in the beginning of accounting studies, point 1 and 2 above are combined and just referred to a single term as “Sales”.
Expenses Debit Credit The business always enjoys if its resources are increased, more building area, machine hours, labor hours, experts’ hours, insurances, bank services, stationaries, equipment, etc.

While students confuse here that expenses are bad, but it is not. Expenses are resources which is benefit for the business. “Paying for those Expenses” lead to decrease in Cash or Asset, which is loss.

More resources you put in the business, more performances arise in generation of revenues through best product and services. As resources are scarce, it is expenses, and business must pay more and more to acquire sufficient resources.

One other aspect of Expenses is that it reduces Owner’s Equity, that’s why, students think of it as “Loss”.
  • - It is Benefit for the Business.

  • - It is Loss for the Owner

An Accounting System always focuses on business’ benefits while analyzing a transaction, but while making financial statements, the accountant’s considers stakeholders as high priority.
