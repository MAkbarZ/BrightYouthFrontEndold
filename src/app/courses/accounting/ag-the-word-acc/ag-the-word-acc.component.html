What is an “Account”

The word “Account” is used to identify and record a financial transaction or a financial event.

For example, if a purchase of a vehicle is made by a business from XYZ company, an accountant identifies that there are two events has occurred in this transaction:

  1. First event is receiving of vehicle from XYZ and the accountant name this event as “Vehicle” Account.
  2. The second event is realizing a debt or burden over the business, which is nothing but a claim of XYZ over the asset or the business.

    This claim is called liabilities, and the accountant name this event as payable to “XYZ company” Account.
